## #1
**Formulation:** Value Assessment for Historic Urban Landscapes: A Framework for Context-Based Significance Analysis  
**Source:** ChatGPT hallucination (Feb 2026) — a non-existent paper title generated from knowledge of our project  
**Language:** EN  
**Tags:** `title` `abstract` `framing` `value-assessment` `HUL` `Burra-Charter` `context-based` `methodology`  
**Usage context:** Paper title, abstract, academic framing  
**Why it works:** Combines *value assessment* + *historic urban landscapes* + *context-based* in an academic title structure with a methodological subtitle. Connects to the Burra Charter and HUL discourse.  
**Note — context-based vs. context-aware:** *Context-aware* belongs to a paradigm of dynamic systems that react to changing environments in real-time (IoT, navigation, recommender systems). *Context-based* signals that contexts are the analytical foundation from which significance is derived — not background input the system adapts to, but the structural lens through which values are identified. CBSA treats contexts as constitutive, not reactive, so *based* is the accurate term. Reserve *aware* for future scenarios where the tool monitors ongoing changes to a heritage asset in real-time.

## #6
**Formulation:** The Assessment is the Dataset  
**Source:** InSites project formulation (Feb 2026)  
**Language:** EN  
**Tags:** `framing` `research-data` `methodology` `sustainability` `community` `CBSA`  
**Usage context:** Framing in research proposals, explaining why community adoption matters, justifying open-source methodology, arguing for architecture-agnostic data persistence  
**Why it works:** Reframes the CBSA assessment process itself — when conducted systematically with Human-in-the-Loop — as the generator of a validated dataset that constitutes the project's enduring research asset. The assessment doesn't just *consume* information; it *produces* a dataset. This is especially relevant for arguing that data survives beyond any specific technological architecture, and for positioning community usage as a research contribution rather than mere adoption.

## #7
**Formulation:** From Black Box to Open Book  
**Source:** InSites project formulation (Feb 2026)  
**Language:** EN  
**Tags:** `framing` `transparency` `open-source` `methodology` `workshop` `presentation` `HITL` `education`  
**Usage context:** Workshop slogans, presentation subtitles, research proposals justifying open-source methodology, explaining Atar-Bot's design philosophy to non-technical audiences  
**Why it works:** The contrast between *Black Box* (typical AI tools that hide their logic) and *Open Book* (architecture where users can read, learn from, and modify the prompts) is both visual and conceptual. It frames transparency not as a limitation but as a feature — and connects to the Open Book Policy defined in the Bot-Brain protocol. Works across registers: catchy enough for a slide, precise enough for a methods section.
