Three grouping schemes emerge from the data:
By significance type:

Event-memorial sites — Beatitudes, Tabgha, Mt. Tabor, Kanna. Primary significance = "this is where X happened." The site exists to commemorate a Gospel event.
Place-as-character sites — Capernaum, Magdala, Chorazin. The place itself figures in the narrative — a town Jesus lived in, worked from, condemned. Significance is about the place, not just what happened there.

By heritage character:

Archaeological-dominant — Capernaum, Chorazin, Magdala. The remains are the site. Significance is carried by physical evidence.
Commemoration-dominant — Beatitudes, Tabgha, Mt. Tabor. Modern or reconstructed churches mark a tradition. Significance is carried by continuity of belief, not primarily by remains.
Living-heritage — Kanna stands alone. A functioning village where sacred and everyday overlap.

By landscape relationship:

Lakeshore cluster — Capernaum, Tabgha, Magdala, Beatitudes (all within a few km on the N/NW shore). Landscape is shared and interdependent.
Hilltop isolates — Chorazin (basaltic spur), Mt. Tabor (Jezreel Valley dome). Landscape is the experience.
Valley-embedded — Kanna. Nested in a living agricultural-village context.
